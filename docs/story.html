Statistical note: Pearson correlations computed at ward level (n varies by analysis). Correlations below |r| = 0.1 treated as negligible. East Midlands excluded from regional chart (only 14 Labour candidates — insufficient sample).

Methodology & data sources

Gender assignment

Around 77% of candidates had no gender recorded in the Democracy Club source data. Gender was predicted algorithmically in three stages:

  1. gender_guesser library — an open-source name database covering common first names internationally. Applied first; produces “high” or “medium” confidence results.
  2. ONS historical baby names (1904–2024) — used for names not resolved by stage 1, and where a birth year was available. Year-aware lookup accounts for names whose gender balance has shifted over time (e.g. “Ashley”, “Kim”).
  3. Claude Sonnet 4.6 (AI) — used for the remaining ~4,000 names unresolved by stages 1 or 2. The model was asked to classify first names by likely gender based on cultural and linguistic context.

63 candidates (<0.3%) remain unclassified. All gender percentages are calculated only among candidates with a known gender.

Gender is treated as binary (male/female) for prediction purposes and is not self-identified gender.

Incumbency

A candidate is classified as an incumbent if they were a sitting councillor in the same ward and council as of 2025. Three conditions must all be met:

  1. Council match — the 2026 council name is matched to a council entry in the 2025 sitting councillor data (from opencouncildata.co.uk, scraped May 2026). Names are normalised by stripping prefixes and suffixes before matching.
  2. Ward match — the 2026 ward name is matched against the 2025 ward names. Exact match is tried first; if that fails, fuzzy matching (difflib.get_close_matches, cutoff 0.6) is attempted, restricted to wards sharing the same first word to prevent cross-area false positives from boundary reorganisations.
  3. Name match — the SOPN name is compared against sitting councillors in the matched ward using difflib.SequenceMatcher. A match is accepted at ratio ≥ 0.80, handling middle names and minor spelling differences.

316 councils were scraped from opencouncildata.co.uk; 154 councils in the 2026 data are matched against these. Incumbents who chose not to re-stand are not counted — “Defeated” covers only those who stood and lost.

Election type (full vs partial)

Election type is read from the Election Model field in the opencouncildata.co.uk dataset. Of the 154 councils, 150 were matched; the 4 unmatched councils (East Surrey, West Surrey, Newport City, Powys) are newly created or Welsh authorities shown as “Unknown”.

Whole-council elections (all seats at once) are marked Full; by-thirds and by-halves elections are marked Partial.
